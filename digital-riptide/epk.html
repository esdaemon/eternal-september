DIGITAL RIPTIDE MEMORY PALACE

DIGITAL RIPTIDE MEMORY PALACE centers around a live recording of a show that never happened.

In the world of Eternal September, Digital Riptide was a promising '80's no wave band from New York City.

Digital Riptide featured dueling saxophones, multiple vintage synths, fretless bass, and electronic drums.

Their song Pink Twilight was released as a single, along with a video featuring a Lite Brite face mask.

This is an excerpt of that video:

The band was fronted by two singers: Liera and Gordon Voidwell. They were in love. Then it all fell apart.

Their last show took place at The Pyramid Club in NYC at the height of Digital Riptide's musical powers.

The set they played that night in 1986 mirrored the rise and fall of Leira and Gordon's own relationship.

The show was reviewed by the Village Voice. Posters were printed for the show. Band t-shirts were sold.

"Live at the Pyramid", recorded via the venue's soundboard, includes the sound of audience interaction.

A limited run of cassettes were printed by White Columns, the band's label. They were put into storage.

Digital Riptide was interviewed by a fan with a camcorder following the show in the Pyramid's greenroom.

After the break-up, Gordon Voidwell went solo, releasing experimental disco music with synths and strings.

Liera formed a band called Metal Swans featuring three saxophones, fretless bass, and electronic drums.

Metal Swans debuted on the public access show Phantom Access TV two years after Digital Riptide's demise.

The remaining members of Digital Riptide went on to form the power electronics trio Black Screen of Death.

Black Screen of Death paired their brutal noise with experimental film footage from the 1960's and '70's.

The Memory Palace presents all these elements - music, video, objects, photos - as an installation.

At the center of this installation is the 1986 recording itself: Digital Riptide's "Live at the Pyramid".

At the center of that album is the song "Into the Turquoise" - a reference to a black hole's event horizon.

At the center of that song is an infinite emptiness surrounded by waves of nostalgia in all directions.
