Narcotics [related: Narcissus, Nepenthe]
Any psychoactive compound known to induce sleepiness, forgetfulness, sometimes accompanied by mild euphoria1. The term was originally taken from narcissus, a narcotic known to induce trance-like states and hallucinations, leading to the etymology of the term "narcissism2" after the myth of an intoxicated youth that drowned after falling in love with his own reflection in a shallow pool of fetid water. The term "narcotic" was later also applied to nepenthe 3, a narcotic of unknown origin described at length in the writings of ancient Greece.

1Debate continues to surround the "source" of narcotic-induced euphoria, with some experts attributing the occasional appearance of euphoria in narcotic users to temporary freedom from the Labyrinth via loss of discrete "self" (as opposed to acute chemo-neurological processes in the brain).

2It should be noted that severe (often impermanent) narcissism often accompanies the onset of schizophrenic and/or non-specific psychotic episodes, a commonality that, according to Buddhist doctrine, can be attributed to the ailing mind being entirely pervaded by an errant sense of discrete, inherently existing "self".

3Nepenthe, a genus of carnivorous plant found throughout the Old World tropics, was later named after the mythical drug, in part based on its technique of intoxicating small lizards and other reptiles into submission and then drowning them in dew-filled monkey cups.
