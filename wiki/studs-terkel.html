Studs Terkel
Louis "Studs" Terkel, a cigar-smoking American radio broadcaster and amateur historian, is best known for his plodding 1000-page [small print] book Working1. Blacklisted for his leftist beliefs during the Age of McCarthyism, Terkel later sued Ma Bell to stop the communication conglomerate's unauthorized release of phone records to the newly formed FBI, a practice instigated covertly during the United States government's no-holds-barred pursuit of the well-dressed, charismatic gangster John Herbert Dillinger and his gang of miscreants.

1An painfully dry and seemingly random series of occupationally-oriented interviews.
