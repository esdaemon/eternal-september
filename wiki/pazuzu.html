Pazuzu
The king demon of the wind in Assyrian and Mesopotamian culture, Pazuzu is depicted as having the body of a man, the head of a lion or dog, talons of an eagle, two pairs of wings, a scorpion's tail and a serpentine penis. Pazuzu is most often shown with his right hand up and left hand down and was characterized in the Grimoire of Solomon as possessing both positive and negative traits. Failure to appease Pazuzu results in famine and locust swarms. The demon's snake-like coiled phallus is seen by many biblical scholars as relating to the Exile from Paradise. In simplest terms, Pazuzu is necessary evil - the spirit of evil charged with driving other more destructive forces1 away.
