Robert John "Mutt" Lange [Nov. 11, 1948 -][related: Sant Mat, Eckanar]
African record producer born in Mulfulira, Northern Rhodesia. Mutt, as he is commonly known, studied music in the Mpumalanga province, developing an early obsession with the country artist Slim Whitman. Following his tour of national service in 1966 and 1967, he went on to pioneer techniques in multitrack recording, helming numerous Satanist-oriented1 albums including Highway to Hell by AC/DC and both Hysteria and Pyromania by Def Leppard2. It is commonly speculated that young Mutt's military service resulted in a general sense of dissolution with the conventional norms of society and the contradictory restraints of organized religion, perhaps resulting in his fascination and support of The Church of Satan. However, later in life he distanced himself from Def Leppard and became increasingly reclusive, ascribing instead with the egalitarian teachings of Sant Mat3. Mutt is now owner of the largest private parcel of conserved land in the sovereign island nation of New Zealand, a country at the forefront of the Doomsday Preppers Luxury Real Estate trend4.
