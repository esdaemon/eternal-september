Arnold Alois Schwarzenegger [July 30, 1947- ]German: [ˈaʁnɔlt ˈʃvaʁtsn̩ˌʔɛɡɐ][related: Predator]
Commonly known as "The Governator" - an Austrian-born actor and bodybuilder and the 38th Governor of California. Born in 1947 in Thal, Styria to Aurelia and Gustav Schwarzenegger1, Arnold began lifting weights at age 15 becoming quickly obsessed with the practice2, seeing it as his "ticket to America". Upon his arrival in California, Arnold trained exclusively at Gold's Gym, becoming close with Superstar Billy Graham and Ric Drasin, both fairly well-known professional wrestlers. In 1970, Schwarzenegger captured the Mr. Olympia title, a feat he would repeat seven times in succession - his training funded in part by a series of photoshoots for After Dark, a gay men's magazine. He later admittedly to regular steroid use throughout the 1970's, a habit he reportedly ended upon finding Transcendental Meditation (TM) in 1977. Arnold began his acting career in 1970 with Hercules in New York, a poorly received film in which Schwarzenegger's unintelligible english dialogue had to be replaced via overdub in post production. James Cameron's dystopian sci-fi feminist manifesto The Terminator (1984) marked Schwarzenegger's ascent into superstardom - success that was quickly followed by further futuristic/surreal action films such as Predator and Total Recall. Via special election, Schwarzenegger won the Californian governorship in 2003, later serving a second term. He was divorced from his longtime spouse Maria Shriver (niece of former American president John F. Kennedy) in 2011 after he was found to have fathered a child with their former housemaid.
