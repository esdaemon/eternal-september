The Moldau [related: Bedřich Smetana]
Placid and listlessly mellifluous symphonic tone poem by the 18th-century Czech composer Bedřich Smetana. Smetana composed The Moldau in complete silence during an extended period of debilitating misophonia1 that led eventually to full deafness in both ears (and, subsequently, a state of violent mania for which the composer was later forcibly institutionalized until his death).

1Condition in which the subject experiences psychotic levels of anger and/or sexual arousal when exposed to sounds made by other humans, esp. lip-smacking, breathing heavily, and other mouth sounds (esp. re: sex stuff).
