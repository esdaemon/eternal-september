Marduk [related: The Prophecy of Marduk, House of the Exorcist] [disambiguation: The Exorcist]
The patron deity of Babylon, Marduk's ascent parallels the transition of Babylon from a peripheral Mesopotamian city-state to the center of an expansive and sophisticated empire. The Babylonian's reverence of Marduk bordered on the maniacal and developed into the first documented system of monotheism in the ancient world, a process inextricably linked to the evolution of uni-camerality1. The Prophecy of Marduk, a purported vaticinium et eventu text dating from around 700BC, outlines Babylon's brutal fall2 to the Elamites and its subsequent emancipation under the leadership of a Great King later revealed to be Nebuchadnezzar II3. The prophecy's narrative is told through the personification of the Statue of Marduk, a precious idol captured and reclaimed multiple times in the millennium leading up to the arrival of Christ.
