In America

Oh Soraya Sarhaddi Nelson
what I am to do with you

all wrapped up and snuggled
in the butchered-by-feathers
morning?

Eye twinkles still twinkling1

Perhaps I can cook fish with you
somewhere in the Arab world.

I pour out some OJ2

For all the things I'll never do.
For the nothingness I’ve become.

For the fact that they used to use
shock treatment. In America.
 That it was a real and true option.

There is no one to blame for
all these problems. Not even
me and my mulch truck.


“The Papers” is always personified.

And it is for that very reason that I
talk to them and ask them
to stop. They are friends with
my phone and our mean
mail lady.

(She buzzes until you answer - even
when covered in birds!)


I call myself a Patriot when I wince in airports
as the crippled car drives by filled
with late businessmen. I long to clean
the world, yelling “I Object!” at
each and every intercom.


(The very thought of ominous voices,
so Soviet3.)


-<REFRESH>-


In my mind's eye: an endless chasm
an infinitude of ambiguous banquets
replete with the Banquet Beer4.

Therein is no endgame. No
dispersion of anathema.

Like a broken Marshall stack
that lies perpetually in waiting.

A scream of joy and/or
circumstance

inferred.


Not real.


But not
yet


ghostly.
