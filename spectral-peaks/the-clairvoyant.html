The Clairvoyant

I.

Some days the whole world seems to belong to The

Gloaming Hour as seen through my unicorn eyes

(a set of material that excludes only Ice Capades
and Balki from Perfect Strangers)


Is Sugar Loaf really a place? - I can’t help but ask -

I’d really like to think that I could dance there.


II.

Condors were such a factor in youth. Before the paradigm shift
of blue swimsuits too tight but made for racing. And now it takes a sea
of anemones in perfect flame to buzz my bonnet.


"I’m drunk when I say I’m drunk
but I am cinnamon always"


III.

Dear Sun: my are you haphazard!


Are Japanese websites made up or real? A certain percentage of things
is only there to trick us.


On the tip of the horizon I see a silver battleship. Whether or not it’s a figment
I can’t be sure. But I know who it’s come for and I know where
it’s going.



IV.

There’s a moment before my phone rings where I can hear it1
and I don’t know how to account for this

or for how often I see three-legged dogs
or phone booths with no phone.

There are seabirds far inland today as a way
of explaining. Saying what I hear
to be “sibilance”as they glide
in full consciousness.

They know what it means to be human
by the strangeness of our televised
shows, which can be summarized
under the headings “sex/murder”
and “baking autumn cupcakes”.












1It= the phone’s ring
